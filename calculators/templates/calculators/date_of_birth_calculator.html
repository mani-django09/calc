Understanding Leap Years in Age Calculations

Leap years add an extra day (February 29th) every four years to keep our calendar aligned with Earth's orbit. When you calculate age from date of birth, leap years must be considered. If you were born on February 29th, you technically only have a "real" birthday every four years, though legally you age on February 28th (or March 1st in some jurisdictions) during non-leap years. Our date of birth calculator handles these special cases automatically, ensuring accurate age calculation regardless of your birth date.

Age Calculation Across Different Cultures

While our calculator uses the Western (Gregorian) calendar, it's worth noting that different cultures calculate age differently. In some East Asian countries, people are considered 1 year old at birth, and everyone ages one year on New Year's Day rather than on their birthday. In Ethiopia, the calendar is different, so ages don't align with Western calculations. Our calculator follows the international standard used in most legal and official contexts worldwide.
